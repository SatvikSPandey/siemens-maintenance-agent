CENTRIFUGAL PUMP MAINTENANCE MANUAL

VIBRATION ISSUES:
Excessive vibration in centrifugal pumps is commonly caused by bearing wear,
misalignment, or cavitation. Bearings should be inspected every 6 months.
Replace bearings if vibration exceeds 4.5mm/s. Check shaft alignment using
a dial indicator. Cavitation occurs when inlet pressure drops below vapor pressure.

TEMPERATURE ISSUES:
High operating temperature indicates bearing failure or inadequate lubrication.
Normal operating temperature range is 40-80 degrees Celsius. If temperature
exceeds 90 degrees, shut down immediately and inspect bearings and seals.

ROUTINE MAINTENANCE:
Change oil every 2000 operating hours. Inspect seals every 6 months.
Clean strainer monthly. Check coupling alignment quarterly.
